1. The angle of elevation of the top of a tower from a point 20m away from its base is 45°. What is the height of the tower?

A. 20m
B. 40m
C. 20√2m
D. 40√2m
Correct Answer: A. 20m

Using tan(45°) = height / distance ⇒ height = 20m.

2. If the angle of elevation of the sun is 60°, the length of the shadow of a 12m-high tree is:

A. 6√3m
B. 4√3m
C. 12√3m
D. 8√3m
Correct Answer: B. 4√3m

tan(60°) = height / shadow ⇒ √3 = 12 / shadow ⇒ shadow = 12/√3 = 4√3m.

3. From the top of a 90m-high building, the angle of depression of a car is 30°. How far is the car from the building?

A. 30√3m
B. 90√3m
C. 90√3m
D. 60√3m
Correct Answer: C. 90√3m

tan(30°) = height / distance ⇒ 1/√3 = 90 / distance ⇒ distance = 90√3m.

4. A ladder 10m long leans against a wall. If the ladder makes a 30° angle with the ground, how high does it reach the wall?

A. 5m
B. 5√3m
C. 10√3m
D. 10m
Correct Answer: A. 5m

sin(30°) = height / ladder ⇒ 1/2 = height / 10 ⇒ height = 5m.

5. The angle of elevation of a tower changes from 30° to 45° as an observer moves 20m closer. What is the height of the tower?

A. 10(√3 + 1)m
B. 10(√3 + 1)m
C. 20(√3 - 1)m
D. 20(√3 + 1)m
Correct Answer: B. 10(√3 + 1)m

Let height = h.
tan(30°) = h/(x + 20) ⇒ 1/√3 = h/(x + 20)
tan(45°) = h/x ⇒ 1 = h/x ⇒ h = x
Solving, h = 10(√3 + 1)m.
