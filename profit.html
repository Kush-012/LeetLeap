1. A shopkeeper buys an item for $50 and sells it for $60. What is the profit percentage?

A. 10%
B. 20%
C. 25%
D. 30%
Correct Answer: B. 20%

Profit = Selling Price − Cost Price = 60 − 50 = 10. Profit % = (10 ÷ 50) × 100 = 20%.

2. An item is sold for $80 at a loss of 20%. What is the cost price?

A. $90
B. $100
C. $110
D. $120
Correct Answer: B. $100

Let Cost Price = x. Selling Price = 80% of x = 0.8x. Given 0.8x = 80, so x = 80 ÷ 0.8 = 100.
