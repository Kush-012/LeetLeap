1. Find the remainder when 293 is divided by 7?

A. 6
B. 4
C. 2
D. 10
Correct Answer: A. 6

293 ÷ 7 = 41 remainder 6. Using division: 293 = 7 × 41 + 6. We can verify: 7 × 41 = 287, and 293 - 287 = 6.

2. The LCM of two numbers is 105 and their HCF is 7. If one of the numbers is 21, find the difference between the two numbers.

A. 35
B. 14
C. 5
D. 21
Correct Answer: B. 14

Using the formula: HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers. So 7 × 105 = 21 × x, which gives us x = 735/21 = 35. Therefore, the difference = 35 - 21 = 14.

3. Which of the following numbers is divisible by 11?

A. 1234
B. 2728
C. 3456
D. 5678
Correct Answer: B. 2728

Divisibility rule for 11: Alternating sum of digits should be divisible by 11. For 2728: (2+2) - (7+8) = 4-15 = -11, which is divisible by 11.

4. What least value should be assigned to * so that the number 451*603 is exactly divisible by 9?

A. 2
B. 8
C. 5
D. 7
Correct Answer: A. 2

Sum of digits = 4+5+1+*+6+0+3 = 19+*. For divisibility by 9, sum must be divisible by 9. The next multiple of 9 after 19 is 27, so * = 8. But we need the least value: 19+2 = 21, which is divisible by 9.

5. Which of the following numbers is both a perfect square and a perfect cube?

A. 36
B. 125
C. 64
D. 100
Correct Answer: C. 64

64 = 8² (perfect square) and 64 = 4³ (perfect cube). A number is both a perfect square and perfect cube if it's a perfect sixth power. 64 = 2⁶.

6. Find the unit digit of 347

A. 1
B. 7
C. 3
D. 9
Correct Answer: B. 7

Unit digits of powers of 3 follow the pattern: 3¹=3, 3²=9, 3³=27(7), 3⁴=81(1), then repeats. Pattern: 3,9,7,1. Since 47 = 4×11 + 3, the unit digit is the same as 3³, which is 7.

7. How many factors does 72 have?

A. 10
B. 12
C. 14
D. 16
Correct Answer: B. 12

72 = 2³ × 3². Number of factors = (3+1)(2+1) = 4×3 = 12. The factors are: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 9, 12, 18, 24, 36, 72.

8. How many trailing zeros are there in 25!?

A. 5
B. 6
C. 7
D. 8
Correct Answer: B. 6

Trailing zeros = number of times 10 divides n! = min(powers of 2, powers of 5). Powers of 5 in 25! = ⌊25/5⌋ + ⌊25/25⌋ = 5 + 1 = 6.

9. A number when divided by 54 leaves a remainder of 31. Find the remainder when the same number is divided by 27?

A. 4
B. 2
C. 8
D. 13
Correct Answer: A. 4

Let the number be N = 54q + 31. Since 54 = 2 × 27, we have N = 2 × 27q + 31. Now 31 = 27 + 4, so N = 27(2q + 1) + 4. Therefore, remainder when divided by 27 is 4.

10. Which of the following is a twin prime pair?

A. (15, 17)
B. (11, 13)
C. (9, 11)
D. (21, 23)
Correct Answer: B. (11, 13)

Twin primes are pairs of prime numbers that differ by 2. Both 11 and 13 are prime numbers and 13 - 11 = 2. Other options: 15 and 21 are not prime, 9 is not prime.

11. Which pair of numbers are co-prime?

A. (14, 21)
B. (18, 24)
C. (15, 28)
D. (12, 18)
Correct Answer: C. (15, 28)

Co-prime numbers have HCF = 1. HCF(15, 28): 15 = 3×5, 28 = 4×7. No common factors, so HCF = 1. Other options have common factors: HCF(14,21)=7, HCF(18,24)=6, HCF(12,18)=6.

12. Which of the following is a perfect number?

A. 28
B. 12
C. 16
D. 18
Correct Answer: A. 28

A perfect number equals the sum of its proper divisors. Factors of 28: 1, 2, 4, 7, 14, 28. Proper divisors: 1+2+4+7+14 = 28. So 28 is perfect.

13. Find sum of factors of 18?

A. 39
B. 34
C. 13
D. 35
Correct Answer: A. 39

18 = 2¹ × 3². Factors: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18. Sum = 1+2+3+6+9+18 = 39. Formula: Sum = (2²-1)/(2-1) × (3³-1)/(3-1) = 3 × 13 = 39.

14. Three bells toll at intervals of 9, 12, and 15 minutes respectively. All three begin to toll at 8 a.m. At what time will they first toll together again?

A. 11:00 am
B. 10:00 am
C. 8:30 am
D. 10:30 am
Correct Answer: A. 11:00 am

LCM of 9, 12, and 15: 9=3², 12=2²×3, 15=3×5. LCM = 2²×3²×5 = 180 minutes = 3 hours. They will toll together after 3 hours from 8:00 am, which is 11:00 am.

15. If 256X561 is divisible by 11, then what can be the value of 'X'?

A. 3
B. 6
C. 0
D. 8
Correct Answer: A. 3

Alternating sum: (2+6+5+1) - (5+X+6) = 14 - (11+X) = 3-X. For divisibility by 11, this should be 0 or multiple of 11. So 3-X = 0, giving X = 3.
