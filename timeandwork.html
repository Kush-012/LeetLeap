1. A can complete a work in 12 days and B can complete the same work in 18 days. If they work together, in how many days will they complete the work?

A. 7.2 days
B. 8 days
C. 6.5 days
D. 9 days
Correct Answer: A. 7.2 days

A's rate = 1/12 work per day
B's rate = 1/18 work per day
Combined rate = 1/12 + 1/18 = 3/36 + 2/36 = 5/36 work per day
Time = 1 ÷ (5/36) = 36/5 = 7.2 days

2. If 15 men can complete a work in 20 days, how many men are needed to complete the same work in 12 days?

A. 20 men
B. 25 men
C. 18 men
D. 30 men
Correct Answer: B. 25 men

Total work = 15 × 20 = 300 man-days
To complete in 12 days: Required men = 300 ÷ 12 = 25 men
Using inverse proportion: M₁ × D₁ = M₂ × D₂
15 × 20 = M₂ × 12
M₂ = 300/12 = 25 men

3. A and B together can complete a work in 8 days. A alone can complete it in 12 days. In how many days can B alone complete the work?

A. 20 days
B. 24 days
C. 18 days
D. 16 days
Correct Answer: B. 24 days

Combined rate (A+B) = 1/8 work per day
A's rate = 1/12 work per day
B's rate = (A+B) rate - A's rate = 1/8 - 1/12
B's rate = 3/24 - 2/24 = 1/24 work per day
Therefore, B alone = 24 days

9. A pipe can fill a tank in 6 hours and another pipe can empty it in 8 hours. If both pipes are opened together, in how much time will the tank be filled?

A. 20 hours
B. 24 hours
C. 18 hours
D. 30 hours
Correct Answer: B. 24 hours

Filling pipe rate = +1/6 tank per hour
Emptying pipe rate = -1/8 tank per hour
Net rate = 1/6 - 1/8 = 4/24 - 3/24 = 1/24 tank per hour
Time to fill = 1 ÷ (1/24) = 24 hours
