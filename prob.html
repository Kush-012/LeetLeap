1. If the probability of an event A is 0.3, what is the probability of the complement of A?

A. 0.3
B. 0.7
C. 1.3
D. -0.3
Correct Answer: B. 0.7

For any event A, P(A) + P(A') = 1
Given P(A) = 0.3
Therefore, P(A') = 1 - 0.3 = 0.7
The complement of an event contains all outcomes not in the original event.

2. In a single throw of a fair die, what is the probability of getting an even number?

A. 1/3
B. 1/2
C. 2/3
D. 1/6
Correct Answer: B. 1/2

Sample space = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}
Even numbers = {2, 4, 6}
Number of favorable outcomes = 3
Total outcomes = 6
Probability = 3/6 = 1/2

3. The probability of drawing a red card from a standard deck of 52 cards is:

A. 1/4
B. 1/2
C. 3/4
D. 1/3
Correct Answer: B. 1/2

Total cards = 52
Red cards (Hearts + Diamonds) = 26
Probability = 26/52 = 1/2
Half the deck consists of red cards (hearts and diamonds).

4. Two events A and B are mutually exclusive if:

A. P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B)
B. P(A ∩ B) = 0
C. P(A|B) = P(A)
D. P(A) × P(B) = 1
Correct Answer: B. P(A ∩ B) = 0

Mutually exclusive events cannot occur simultaneously.
Their intersection is empty, so P(A ∩ B) = 0
Example: Getting heads and tails in a single coin flip.
When events are mutually exclusive, P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B)

5. Two events A and B are independent if:

A. P(A ∩ B) = 0
B. P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)
C. P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B)
D. P(A) = P(B)
Correct Answer: B. P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)

Independent events: occurrence of one doesn't affect the other
P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B)
Also equivalent to P(A|B) = P(A) and P(B|A) = P(B)
Example: Rolling two dice - outcome of first die doesn't affect second die

6. If P(A) = 0.4, P(B) = 0.3, and A and B are independent events, find P(A ∪ B):

A. 0.7
B. 0.58
C. 0.12
D. 0.46
Correct Answer: B. 0.58

For independent events: P(A ∩ B) = P(A) × P(B) = 0.4 × 0.3 = 0.12
P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)
P(A ∪ B) = 0.4 + 0.3 - 0.12 = 0.58

7. A fair coin is tossed 3 times. What is the probability of getting exactly 2 heads?

A. 1/4
B. 3/8
C. 1/2
D. 1/8
Correct Answer: B. 3/8

Sample space: {HHH, HHT, HTH, HTT, THH, THT, TTH, TTT}
Favorable outcomes (exactly 2 heads): {HHT, HTH, THH}
Number of favorable outcomes = 3
Total outcomes = 8
Probability = 3/8

8. Two coins are tossed simultaneously. What is the probability of getting at least one head?

A. 1/2
B. 2/3
C. 3/4
D. 1/4
Correct Answer: C. 3/4

Sample space: {HH, HT, TH, TT}
At least one head: {HH, HT, TH}
OR use complement: P(at least one head) = 1 - P(no heads)
P(no heads) = P(TT) = 1/4
P(at least one head) = 1 - 1/4 = 3/4

9. A biased coin has probability 0.6 of showing heads. If tossed twice, what is the probability of getting exactly one head?

A. 0.36
B. 0.48
C. 0.6
D. 0.24
Correct Answer: B. 0.48

P(H) = 0.6, P(T) = 0.4
Exactly one head can occur in two ways: HT or TH
P(HT) = 0.6 × 0.4 = 0.24
P(TH) = 0.4 × 0.6 = 0.24
P(exactly one head) = 0.24 + 0.24 = 0.48

Number of favorable outcomes = 6
Probability = 6/36 = 1/6

12. A die is rolled twice. What is the probability that the second roll is higher than the first?

A. 1/3
B. 5/12
C. 1/2
D. 7/12
Correct Answer: B. 5/12

Total outcomes = 36
Count favorable cases where second > first:
First=1: second can be 2,3,4,5,6 (5 cases)
First=2: second can be 3,4,5,6 (4 cases)
First=3: second can be 4,5,6 (3 cases)
First=4: second can be 5,6 (2 cases)
First=5: second can be 6 (1 case)
Total favorable = 5+4+3+2+1 = 15
Probability = 15/36 = 5/12

13. From a standard deck of 52 cards, what is the probability of drawing a face card (Jack, Queen, King)?

A. 1/4
B. 3/13
C. 1/13
D. 9/52
Correct Answer: B. 3/13

Total cards = 52
Face cards per suit = 3 (Jack, Queen, King)
Total face cards = 4 suits × 3 = 12
Probability = 12/52 = 3/13

14. Two cards are drawn from a deck without replacement. What is the probability that both are aces?

A. 1/221
B. 1/169
C. 4/52
D. 1/13
Correct Answer: A. 1/221

P(first ace) = 4/52
P(second ace | first ace) = 3/51
P(both aces) = (4/52) × (3/51) = 12/2652 = 1/221
Without replacement, the second probability depends on the first outcome.

15. A bag contains 3 red and 5 blue balls. Given that a blue ball is drawn, what is the probability that it was drawn from the bag on the second draw (if the first ball drawn was red and not replaced)?

A. 3/7
B. 5/7
C. 5/8
D. 3/8
Correct Answer: B. 5/7

This is conditional probability. Given first ball was red:
Remaining balls = 2 red + 5 blue = 7 balls
P(blue on second draw | first was red) = 5/7
Since first ball was red and removed, 5 blue balls remain out of 7 total remaining balls.
