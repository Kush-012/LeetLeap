1. What is the simple interest on $1000 at 5% per annum for 2 years?

A. $50
B. $100
C. $150
D. $200
Correct Answer: B. $100

Simple Interest = (Principal × Rate × Time) ÷ 100 = (1000 × 5 × 2) ÷ 100 = 100.

2. A sum of $5000 earns $750 simple interest in 3 years. What is the rate of interest per annum?

A. 4%
B. 4.5%
C. 5%
D. 6%
Correct Answer: C. 5%

Simple Interest = (P × R × T) ÷ 100. So, 750 = (5000 × R × 3) ÷ 100. Solving, R = (750 × 100) ÷ (5000 × 3) = 5%.

3. How many years will it take for $2000 to earn $400 simple interest at 4% per annum?

A. 3 years
B. 4 years
C. 5 years
D. 6 years
Correct Answer: C. 5 years

Simple Interest = (P × R × T) ÷ 100. So, 400 = (2000 × 4 × T) ÷ 100. Solving, T = (400 × 100) ÷ (2000 × 4) = 5 years.

4. What is the compound interest on $1000 at 10% per annum for 2 years, compounded annually?

A. $200
B. $210
C. $210
D. $220
Correct Answer: C. $210

Amount = P(1 + R/100)^T = 1000(1 + 10/100)^2 = 1000 × 1.1^2 = 1210. Compound Interest = 1210 − 1000 = 210.

5. A sum of $2000 amounts to $2420 in 2 years at compound interest, compounded annually. What is the rate of interest?

A. 8%
B. 9%
C. 10%
D. 11%
Correct Answer: C. 10%

Amount = P(1 + R/100)^T. So, 2420 = 2000(1 + R/100)^2. Then, (1 + R/100)^2 = 2420/2000 = 1.21. Taking square root, 1 + R/100 = 1.1, so R = 10%.

7. A sum amounts to $1331 in 3 years at 10% per annum compound interest, compounded annually. What is the principal?

A. $900
B. $1000
C. $1100
D. $1200

Amount = P(1 + R/100)^T. So, 1331 = P(1 + 10/100)^3 = P × 1.1^3 = P × 1.331. Thus, P = 1331 ÷ 1.331 = 1000.

10. A sum doubles in 5 years at simple interest. What is the rate of interest per annum?

A. 15%
B. 18%
C. 20%
D. 25%
Correct Answer: C. 20%

Let Principal = P. Amount = 2P. Simple Interest = 2P − P = P. So, P = (P × R × 5) ÷ 100. Solving, R = 100 ÷ 5 = 20%.

11. What is the difference between compound interest and simple interest on $10000 at 5% per annum for 2 years?

A. $25
B. $25
C. $50
D. $75
Correct Answer: B. $25

Simple Interest = (10000 × 5 × 2) ÷ 100 = 1000. Compound Interest = 10000 × (1 + 5/100)^2 − 10000 = 10000 × 1.05^2 − 10000 = 1025. Difference = 1025 − 1000 = 25.

15. A sum of $10000 is invested at 5% per annum simple interest for 3 years and at 5% per annum compound interest for 3 years, compounded annually. What is the difference in interest earned?

A. $37.50
B. $38.25
C. $40.00
D. $45.50
Correct Answer: B. $38.25

Simple Interest = (10000 × 5 × 3) ÷ 100 = 1500. Compound Interest = 10000 × (1 + 5/100)^3 − 10000 = 10000 × 1.05^3 − 10000 ≈ 1538.25. Difference = 1538.25 − 1500 = 38.25.
